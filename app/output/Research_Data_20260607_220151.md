### New Delhi Capital Status

New Delhi serves as the capital of the Republic of India [1]. Its status as a capital has evolved through various historical and administrative changes:

#### Historical Timeline
*   **1911:** The colonial British government designated Delhi as the capital of India on December 12, 1911 [1, 3]. At that time, it was known as the "Chief Commissioner’s Province" [3].
*   **1950:** New Delhi was established as the capital of the Republic of India on January 26, 1950 [1].

#### Administrative Status
*   **Union Territory:** Following the Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act and the States Re-Organization Act of 1956, Delhi was designated as a Union Territory governed by an administrator appointed by the President [1, 3].
*   **National Capital Territory (NCT):** As of February 1, 1992, Delhi is officially recognized as the National Capital Territory [1].

#### Governance
*   The territory is governed by the Government of Delhi [1].
*   It features a unicameral legislature consisting of 70 seats [1].
*   The administration includes a Lieutenant Governor and a Chief Minister [1].
