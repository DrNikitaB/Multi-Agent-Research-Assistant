Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) is an artificial intelligence architecture that optimizes the output of large language models (LLMs) by connecting them to external, authoritative knowledge bases [2, 4]. 

Instead of relying solely on the static, pre-existing training data that an LLM was originally built with, RAG enables the model to retrieve and incorporate up-to-date or domain-specific information before generating a response [1, 2].

### How RAG Works
The RAG process functions by blending the LLM's generative capabilities with an information retrieval process [1, 5]. The workflow generally follows these steps:
* **Retrieval:** When a user submits a query, the system searches external sources—such as internal company documents, databases, scholarly journals, or web sources—to find relevant information [1, 2].
* **Augmentation:** The retrieved information is then provided to the LLM as context alongside the user's original query [1, 4].
* **Generation:** The LLM uses this supplemental context to generate a more accurate, relevant, and fact-based response [1, 2].

### Why RAG is Important
LLMs are often compared to "over-enthusiastic" employees who may answer questions with absolute confidence even when they are incorrect [4]. RAG addresses several inherent limitations of standard LLMs:
* **Reduces AI Hallucinations:** By grounding responses in authoritative sources, RAG helps prevent models from inventing facts, policies, or nonexistent legal cases [1, 4].
* **Overcomes Knowledge Cutoffs:** LLMs have a "knowledge cutoff," meaning their training data is static and becomes outdated over time. RAG allows models to access real-time information [2, 4].
* **Domain-Specific Accuracy:** RAG enables organizations to use their own proprietary or internal data to provide specialized answers without the need for expensive model retraining [1, 2].
* **Transparency:** Because RAG systems can include citations from the retrieved documents, users can verify the accuracy of the information provided [1, 4].

### Benefits for Organizations
Implementing RAG offers several operational advantages:
* **Cost-Efficiency:** Retraining or fine-tuning foundation models is computationally and financially expensive. RAG provides a more cost-effective way to adapt models to specific domains [1, 2, 4].
* **Scalability:** Organizations can scale their AI applications by connecting them to new data sources as needed without the resource-intensive process of full model retraining [2].
* **Improved Trust:** By providing more accurate, verifiable, and current information, RAG helps increase user trust in AI-powered chatbots and NLP applications [2, 4].

### Limitations
While RAG improves performance, it is not a perfect solution. LLMs can still generate misinformation if they misinterpret the context of the retrieved information. For example, a model might retrieve a factually correct document but fail to understand the rhetorical context of a title or statement, leading to a false conclusion [1].
