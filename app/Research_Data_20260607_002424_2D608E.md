### What is Agentic AI?

Agentic AI refers to artificial intelligence systems designed with autonomy and decision-making capabilities, allowing them to interpret data, learn from interactions, and drive actions toward specific goals with minimal human intervention [2, 3, 4]. 

The term "agentic" is derived from psychology and education, describing the ability to independently take initiative and drive outcomes [5]. Unlike traditional AI, which is limited to specific tasks, or generative AI, which is primarily focused on creating content, agentic AI is capable of executing actions in the real world or within digital ecosystems to achieve higher-level objectives [2, 4, 5].

### How Agentic AI Works

Agentic AI functions by acting as an overarching system that coordinates and manages multiple "AI agents" to handle complex workflows [4, 5]. Its operation can be broken down into several key components and processes:

*   **The "Brain" (LLMs):** Modern agentic AI leverages the reasoning capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs) to handle novel situations, plan multiple steps, and determine the best way to answer a question or solve a problem [2, 3, 4].
*   **Orchestration:** The system uses orchestration—often through flows or graphs—to provide the LLM with access to external tools and algorithms. This allows the system to decide, for example, whether it has enough information to answer a query or if it needs to perform an external search [3, 4].
*   **AI Agents:** These are the building blocks of the system. They are individual software entities designed to perform specific, specialized tasks, such as resetting a password, executing code, or retrieving a file [3, 4, 5].
*   **Environment Interaction:** Agentic AI operates within digital ecosystems (such as APIs, web applications, and operating systems) and is evolving toward physical environments through robotics and sensory integration [2, 3].
*   **Autonomy and Reasoning:** Unlike static automation, agentic AI can adjust its behavior based on new information. It perceives its environment, processes observations, and makes decisions in real time to adapt to changing workflows [2, 5].

### Key Distinctions

*   **Agentic AI vs. Generative AI:** While generative AI creates content (text, images, code), agentic AI uses generative AI as a tool to perform actions in underlying systems to achieve broader goals [4].
*   **Agentic AI vs. AI Agents:** "AI agents" are the individual tools or entities that perform specific tasks, whereas "agentic AI" describes the complete, coordinated system that manages these agents to achieve complex objectives [3, 4, 5].

### Evolution of the Technology
The development of agentic AI has progressed through several stages:
1.  **Text processing models:** Traditional LLMs that generate text but lack interaction capabilities [2].
2.  **Language agents:** Systems that integrate LLMs with external environments to process observations and take actions [2].
3.  **Cognitive AI agents:** The most advanced form, which manages internal reasoning processes to plan and adapt dynamically [2].
